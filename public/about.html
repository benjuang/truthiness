About Us

Truthiness: A quality characterizing a "truth" that a person claims to know intuitively "from the gut" or because it "feels right" without regard to evidence, logic, intellectual examination, or facts.

Truthiness, the website: A popular science website aimed at distinguishing myth from fact, with articles, quizzes, fora, links, etc.!

Our mission is to make science issues that are relevant to us in everyday life more understandable. Not everyone is a scientist, and that's ok. But sometimes, a lack of understanding of scientific principles can make it hard to understand discussions on important topics, such as climate change or genetically modified organisms. There are a lot of myths floating around about these topics. A lot of times, people who don't have a solid foundation in science can make claims in an authoritative manner, which can confuse people who haven't really thought about the issue! This website aims to present the facts so that you can make informed decisions.

We do have opinions on the topics. Both sides of an issue are not always equally valid; often, one side is supported by science while the other is not. We won't get political, but we will share our opinions based on the facts.

We've thought a lot about why people are willing to believe claims made by people who don't understand how science works (think people who don't accept that current climate changes are precipitated by humans, or Todd Akin's claim that women don't get pregnant during "legitimate rape" because their bodies can just "shut that whole thing down." We think it's because a lot of people feel like the fact that doctors and scientists have made mistakes in the past means they're not trustworthy now. What's important to remember, though, is that everybody can make mistakes, but only some people are willing to learn from those mistakes. The people to listen to are the ones who accept evidence as a reason to change.

We can't just make things up and pretend they're true. We have to look at the facts! So go through this website, read some articles, and take a couple quizzes. Hopefully, the things you see will help you make informed decisions and understand issues that affect so many people.
