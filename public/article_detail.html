Food History

Many fruits and vegetables that we eat today have been greatly modified by humans over the past centuries or millennia! An excellent example is corn, the number one field crop in America.

Seven millennia ago, people living in central Mexico started harvesting a wild grass known as teosinte, which had small, spread-out kernels that were a far cry from the densely nutritious ears of corn that we see today.

Farmers artificially manipulated this grass through careful cultivation to the corn we are familiar with today.

Left: teosinte; center: maize (corn)/teosinte hybrid; right: maize.

Today’s product is dependent on human aid for reproduction, because the dense packing of kernels does not permit the natural reproductive processes to take place.

The rate of change from teosinte to today’s corn is far, far greater than the natural rate of evolution. Even though we do not generally think of today’s corn as a genetically modified organism (GMO), it is! Without human intervention, if the transformation to today’s corn had ever taken place, it certainly would’ve taken much, much longer.
