What is genetic modification?

Genetic modification is the changing of genetic code by adding or removing lengths of DNA. Such modifications can change the properties of the organism, such as resistance to pests, pesticides, or hard conditions; extended shelf life; production of useful chemicals in the plant; and increased nutritional value.

Food history

Many fruits and vegetables that we eat today have been greatly modified by humans over the past centuries or millennia. An excellent example is corn, the number one field crop in America. Learn more about corn.

Label laws

The GMOs that are currently being contested or discussed throughout America and the world, as in California’s Prop 37, follow the same idea. Would you expect labels proclaiming genetic modification on the corn and bananas that you currently buy at your local grocery store? See what the laws are all about.
